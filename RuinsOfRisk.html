Elevator Pitch: Ruins of Risk is a third-person parkour game where the player takes on the role of a monk on a mission to collect powerful artifacts and save the world from a malevolent force. With a focus on challenging gameplay, immersive environments, and intense moments of danger, Ruins of Risk will test your skills and reflexes as you navigate the treacherous ruins of an ancient temple.
Summary of Core Gameplay: Ruins of Risk is a challenging parkour game that combines fast-paced action with platforming elements. Players take on the role of a monk on a mission to collect powerful artifacts and defeat a malevolent force that threatens the world. With a variety of traps, obstacles, and enemies standing in your way, the gameplay is designed to be both challenging and rewarding. You'll need to master your movement abilities, including walking, sprinting, jumping, crouching, and climbing over obstacles, to navigate the ruins and collect the artifacts.



Gameplay and Level Design


 Ruins of Risk features a third-person camera perspective that allows players to fully immerse themselves in the game world. The game takes place in the ruins of an ancient temple, with each level featuring unique challenges and obstacles to overcome. The level design is focused on creating a sense of danger and urgency, with traps and hazards that will test your reflexes and timing. For example, wind zones will blow players away, while falling in water results in instant death and a respawn at the starting point.

The ludonarrative resonance design problem is tackled in Ruins of Risk by removing the player's ability to reload from a recent checkpoint, creating a sense of danger and fear of losing progress. As the player progresses through the game, the challenges become more difficult, increasing the fear of dying and the desire to succeed.

Project Goal: The goal of the project was to create a challenging parkour game that combined fast-paced action with immersive environments and intense moments of danger. The focus was on creating a sense of ludonarrative resonance, where players feel connected to their character and experience the danger and urgency of the game world.

Challenges and Solutions:
  • One of the biggest challenges of the project was creating a level design that was both challenging and fair. To solve this, we used a playtesting approach where we continually tweaked the level design based on player feedback to ensure a balance between difficulty and playability.
  • Another challenge was implementing the ludonarrative resonance design approach, which required a careful balance between creating a sense of danger and frustration for players. This was solved by testing and iterating on the checkpoint system to create a balance between difficulty and player progression.
